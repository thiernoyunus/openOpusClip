# How To Make A Podcast Teaser Trailer (Like Diary Of A CEO)

## TL;DR

- This tutorial explains DOAC-style podcast trailers as a story framework first, not an effects recipe: hook, value, authority, emotional roller coaster or more value, then cliffhanger.
- The sample edit uses yellow subtitle blocks, manual caption cuts, big pop-out text, blurred backgrounds, subject cutouts, icons, graphics, sweep transitions, whooshes, risers, and small SFX.
- Caption advice is practical: generate captions with AI, correct them manually, cut them into short readable chunks, then add separate dynamic text for the biggest words.
- Blur and depth are used to make flat podcast footage feel cinematic: blur the background, keep the speaker sharp, and place emphasis text/graphics in the visual gap.
- B-roll is brand-dependent: polished brands can use motion graphics/icons; more corporate brands should use restrained b-roll and simple text while keeping the story structure.

- **[05:13] Cliffhanger** — Ends by opening a loop and leaving it unresolved, so people need the full episode.

- **[23:15] Sound effects** — Adds whooshes, risers, and small SFX when things slide, fade, rise, or move quickly on screen.

## Concepts surfaced

- DOAC trailer framework: hook, value, authority, emotional roller coaster or extra value, cliffhanger.
- Hook selection: pick a clip that targets the listener's pain point, dream outcome, or curiosity gap.
- Value-first editing: give the viewer a useful idea inside the trailer before asking them to watch more.
- Selective captions: use AI subtitles as a base, but manually split, correct, and animate only what needs attention.
- Pop-out text: create separate text moments for dramatic phrases instead of relying only on subtitle captions.
- Blur depth: blur the background and keep/cut out the subject to fake a more cinematic depth-of-field look.
- Meaningful visuals: add graphics/icons/b-roll that explain the sentence, not generic decoration.
- Motion-matched SFX: add whooshes, risers, and hits only when the screen movement asks for them.
- Brand restraint: use fewer graphics and more clean b-roll when the podcast brand is corporate or less cinematic.
